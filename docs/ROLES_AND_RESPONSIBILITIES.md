# Roles and Responsibilities

Roles are descriptive and voluntary. This document aligns with `/ethics` and
`docs/COMMUNICATIONS_RULES.md`.

## Observer
- Purpose: Review public materials and track updates without obligation.
- Permissions (can): Read public docs and changelogs.
- Permissions (can’t): Represent EIC or make binding decisions.
- Accountability: Observations are self-directed; no action logs required.
- Offboarding: Disengage at any time without penalty.

## Participant
- Purpose: Engage with drafts and provide feedback when invited.
- Permissions (can): Submit feedback through approved channels.
- Permissions (can’t): Issue commitments, claims, or operational directives.
- Accountability: Feedback is recorded with source and date when submitted.
- Offboarding: Stop participation at any time; prior feedback remains archived.

## Contributor (Builder)
- Purpose: Draft content or prototypes under review.
- Permissions (can): Propose changes, submit drafts, and support documentation.
- Permissions (can’t): Merge public changes without maintainer approval.
- Accountability: Contributions are logged in version control or review notes.
- Offboarding: Request removal from active lists; contributions remain in history.

## Steward (Captain)
- Purpose: Coordinate a domain area and maintain neutral posture.
- Permissions (can): Organize reviews, set agendas, and request clarifications.
- Permissions (can’t): Override ethics, freeze, or release gates unilaterally.
- Accountability: Decisions and recommendations are recorded in changelogs.
- Offboarding: Transfer responsibilities and document handoff notes.

## Maintainer (Code/Docs)
- Purpose: Ensure technical and editorial integrity of the repository.
- Permissions (can): Merge approved changes and manage release hygiene.
- Permissions (can’t): Introduce features or messaging outside authorization.
- Accountability: Changes are recorded in commits and changelog entries.
- Offboarding: Hand off access and document pending tasks.

## Security Steward
- Purpose: Monitor risk posture and enforce security-first practices.
- Permissions (can): Require reviews, recommend mitigations, block risky changes.
- Permissions (can’t): Authorize new features without governance approval.
- Accountability: Security reviews are logged with scope and outcome.
- Offboarding: Transfer review artifacts and open risk items.

## Communications Steward
- Purpose: Ensure public statements comply with communications rules.
- Permissions (can): Approve or reject public copy for compliance.
- Permissions (can’t): Publish unreviewed statements or imply endorsements.
- Accountability: Approvals are logged with source, date, and rationale.
- Offboarding: Hand off statement logs and pending reviews.
